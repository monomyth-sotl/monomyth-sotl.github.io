Silence of the Lambs

About the film

Dr. Hannibal Lecter. Brilliant. Cunning. Psychotic. In his mind lies the clue to a ruthless killer.
Clarice Starling, F.B.I. Brilliant. Vulnerable. Alone. She must trust him to stop the killer.

Clarice Starling is a top student of the F.B.I. Academy. Jack Crawford, head of the Behavioural Science department, wants Clarice to interview Dr. Hannibal Lecter, as known as "Hannibal the Cannibal," to get some insight of a case of a serial killer "Buffalo Bill", who kidnaps and skins the vicitms. Dr. Lecter is a violent psychopath who is servind life behind bars for various acts of murder and cannibalism.

The movie "Silence of the Lambs" illustrates Clarice's journey to find Buffalo Bill with the help and assistance of the F.B.I. and Hannibal Lecter.

Will Clarice be able to win the psychological game between the manipulative doctor?







Hero's Jouney

The Hero's Journey is a pattern of the narrative of a broad category of tales and lore that involves a hero who goes on an adventure, and in a decisive crisis wins a victory, and then comes home changed or transformed.













1. Ordinary World/ Status Quo

Clarice Starling is a female senior trainee for the FBI. She has a double major in Psychology and Criminology. When she graduates, she very much wants to work with Crawford at Behavioural Science.


2. The Call to Adventure

Part 1: The Assignment


When Starling enters Jack Crawford’s room, the Head of FBI’s Behavioural Science Services, she notices newspaper articles regarding Buffalo Bill, the mystery serial killer who skins the victims. Soon, Crawford enters the room, and gives Starling an assignment to interview Hannibal Lecter. The Behavioural Science department is interviewing all the serial killers in custody for psycho behavioural profile to help unsolved cases. Most of them are happy to talk with the FBI, but the one FBI wants the most to interview refused to cooperate - the psychiatrist Hannibal Lecter, or Hannibal “the Cannibal”. If Hannibal doesn’t want to cooperate, Crawford just wants straight reporting - how he looks, how his cell looks, if he is sketching, then what is he sketching. Crawford hands documents about Lecter, a copy of the questionnaire and a special ID to the asylum to Starling. Crawford warns Starling to be very careful with Hannibal Lecter, and to exactly follow the physical procedures Dr. Chilton at the asylum will go over those that are used with him. Starling faces beginnings of change in her trainee life.

Part 2: The Visit

Starling arrives at Baltimore State Forensic Hospital to fulfill her assignment. Dr. Chilton, the supervisor of the hospital, notes that the hospital has tried to study Hannibal Lecter in the past, but he was too sophisticated for the standardized tests. While guiding Starling to Hannibal, Dr. Chilton tells Starling the physical procedures she must follow at all costs. He says to not touch or approach the glass cell wall, only use the sliding food carrier, and to only hand him paper - no staples or paperclips.

3. Meeting the Mentor

When Starling and Hannibal make an acquaintance, Hannibal refuses to accept the questionnaire right away. He asks several questions to Starling regarding Buffalo Bill, such as why he is called Buffalo Bill, and what makes him skin people. Starling calmly answers the questions.

Then, Hannibal tells Starling to hand the questionnaire, but is soon disappointed because of the bluntness of the questions to dissect him. Hannibal dissects Starling’s personal details just by her looks, such as where she is from, what family she grew up in. Moment of realization hits Starling that Hannibal is very smart and intelligent. However, Staring provokes Hannibal by saying, “Are you smart enough to point that high-powered perception at yourself? Or maybe you’re afraid to?”. Hannibal returns the questionnaire back to Starling through the food carrier, and mentions that he once ate a liver of a man who tried to test him with wine. He tells Starling, “fly back to school now, little Starling. Fly, fly, fly...” - he realized that Starling isn’t capable of interviewing him?

4. Refusal of the Call

On her way out of the cell, neighbouring criminals(mainly Miggs) violently threaten/ sexually harass Starling. Hannibal yells at Starling to come back, and offers her a chance for advancement. He tells her to visit his past patient, Ms. Mofet. On her way out, Starling reminisces cheerful memories of her dead father, and cries by her car because of the shock and terror she experienced in the Forensic Hospital. She feels shock and fear of the unknown, however briefly.

5. Crossing the Threshold

Part 1: At the F.B.I. Academy

Starling returns to the FBI, and goes back to her daily routine of training and studying. She continues with her investigation, and reads newspaper articles regarding Hannibal Lecter and his past patient, Mofet. Unfortunately, Lecter altered or destroyed the patient's history prior to his capture so Starling hasn’t found the patient yet, but Starling found a self-storage facility in downtown Baltimore, which is where Hannibal is from, called “Your Self Storage”. Starling commits to further investigate Buffalo Bill.

Part 2: The Storage Unit

Starling reaches out to the facility and enters Hester Mofet’s storage unit which was left abandoned for 10 years. Mofet’s unit is filled with stacks of miscellaneous items, and in the corner there sits a car covered with the flag of the US. In the abandoned car of the storage room, Starling finds the head of a man with makeup on in a bottle. Starling goes straight to Baltimore State Forensic Hospital to visit Hannibal. Hannibal says the man is Benjamin Raspail, a former patient of Hannibal who had exotic romantic attachments. Hannibal says that he didn’t kill Raspail, and that his death was the best thing for Raspail because his therapy was going nowhere. Raspail was garden variety manic-depressive, a tedious bipolar disorder symptom. Starling notices that Hannibal’s drawings have been taken down. Hannibal tells that it was a petty torment from Dr. Chilton for making Miggs kill himself, along with the gospel TV program. Hannibal says that he can help the FBI catch Buffalo Bill, by providing a psychological profile of Buffalo Bill based on the case evidence. Starling hesitates.

Part 3: The Evidence

A body of a victim is found near Elk River, West Virginia. Crawford and Starling visit the site. During the examination of the corpse of the victim, Starling finds a cocoon in the victim’s throat. Starling visits a museum to get the researchers to identify the evidence found. The cocoon is identified as Acheronita, a special type of moth that is only found in Asia. Buffalo Bill imported the moth and raised it with care.

Part 4: The Deal

The next day, Catherine Martin, the daughter of Senator Ruth Martin, goes missing. Dr. Chilton is unhappy with the fact that Starling isn’t sharing information obtained from the interview of Hannibal Lecter with the hospital for the third time. He claims that he has rights to the information since Hannibal is his patient. Starling refuses to do so, but rather gives him the phone number of the U.S. Attorney’s office and tells him to let her do her job.

Starling goes to Hannibal, and offers a deal to cooperate with the investigation of Buffalo Bill, and in exchange, the Senator has offered to transfer Hannibal to the VA Hospital at Oneida Park, with the view of the woods nearby with maximum security. Also, one week of the year, Hannibal will get to leave the hospital and go to Plum Island under SWAT team surveillance. Of course, the deal is canceled if Catherine Martin dies. Hannibal accepts the offer, and says that if he tells Starling things, Starling should tell him things. He asks about Starling’s worst memories in her childhood. Starling responds that it is when his Starling’s father died from a shooting incident when she was 10 years old. Starling asks Hannibal why Buffalo Bill places the moth cocoon in victims’ throats. Hannibal says that the significance of the moth is ‘change’, and Buffalo Bill wants to change, too. He is not perfectly a transexual, only tries to be. Hannibal suggests that Bill has most likely been rejected to all three major centres that are known to perform sexual reassignment surgery - Johns Hopkins, University of Minnesota and Columbus Medical Centre. Hannibal says that big childhood disturbances associated with violence can cause this. Not a born criminal - a made criminal over years of systematic abuse. Bill hates his own identity and wants sexual transfer, but his pathology is 1000 times more savage and terrifying. Dr. Chilton was listening to the whole conversation between Hannibal and Starling.

Part 4: To Tennessee

Dr. Chilton enters Hannibal’s cell, and tells Hannibal that he himself called Senator Ruth Martin and confirmed that there was no deal regarding Hannibal's transfer. Chilton tells Hannibal to identify Buffalo Bill by name, and if Catherine Martin is found in time, Senator Martin will have Hannibal transferred to Brushy Mountain, Tennessee. Hannibal only tells his first name, and claims that he will remain silent until he arrives at Tennessee. Hannibal focuses on Chilton’s pen to steal it.

6. Tests, Allies and Enemies

Part 1: The Details

The fake deal between Crawford and Hannibal is revealed. The Senator wants the Department of Justice to take over Hannibal from Memphis, Tennessee. Hannibal Lecter and Dr. Chilton arrive at Memphis International Airport. As they arrive, Senator Martin asks for further details of Buffalo Bill. Hannibal says that his real name is Louis Friend. Louis and Raspail were lovers, but Raspail became very frightened because Louis was a murderer and did ‘things’ with his skin. He describes Buffalo Bill’s features - 5’ 10, burnt, about 180 lbs, blonde hair, eyes pale blue, about 35 years old, and may have lied about his address.

Part 2: 'Simplicity'

Starling revisits Hannibal, locked at Shelby County Courthouse. Hannibal says that every information Starling needs is in the case file. He says that the first principle to finding Buffalo Bill is simplicity. What is it in itself? What is its nature? What needs does he serve by his killing? Starling responds, “Anger, social acceptance, sexual frustrations.” Hannibal says that he “covets,” and that is his nature. There are only five minutes left for Starling to interview Hannibal, but Hannibal insists to listen more about the reason why Starling ran away from her cousin’s farm in Montana when she was 10 years old. Starling responded that she heard screams at dawn, so she went downstairs to check the source of the sound. She crept into the barn and realized that it was the lambs. She opened the gate to the pen and attempted to free them, but they just stood there and kept wailing. Starling held onto one lamb and ran away from the farm with no destination in mind. She didn’t get more than a few miles, soon caught by a sheriff, then was sent to an orphanage. The lamb that she ran away with was killed. She mentions that she still has nightmares of this night.

That moment, Dr. Chilton and the security guards enter, and push Starling out the room. Starling pleads Hannibal for Buffalo Bill’s real name, but he won’t answer. He just says to let him know when the lambs stop screaming. As Hannibal hands back the Buffalo Bill case file back to Starling, he gently strokes her hand. Starling, startled, withdraws her hand and is guided out the room. She gets on a plane, and safely returns back to the FBI Academy.

Part 3: The Escape

Hannibal escapes his cell by attacking the two officers who came to give him food. He brutally murders both of them. In the chaos, Hannibal tricks the police, SWAT team and medical staff by laying fake evidence to confuse them locating him. He changes outfits with the dead police and puts on the facial skin layer of the dead officers on his own face and tricks them that he is the injured officer. He gets on the ambulance, and when the medical staff is on the phone to report the severity of the injury back to the hospital, Hannibal attacks him and escapes from the van. Soon, the ambulance is found at a parking garage at Memphis Airport, and the whole crew is dead. Is Hannibal really an ally now?

Part 4: The Evidence

Starling hears the news about Hannibal’s escape from her friend at the FBI Academy, Ardelia. However, Starling knows that Hannibal won’t attack him because he would consider that “rude”, but she thinks that the investigation came to a dead end, and that Catherine Martin will die soon. The two investigate based on the notes Hannibal wrote on the case file he returned back to Starling. The note said, “Clarice, doesn’t this random scattering of the site seem desperately random? Like the elaboration of a bad liar.” Ardelia notices that it is random because of the one girl, Frederica Bimmel, the one Buffalo Bill intentionally weighted down the river. She was the first victim taken, but the third body found. Based on the Lecter’s First Principles, simplicity, and that Buffalo Bill covets, and we covet what we see everyday, they realize that Buffalo Bill knew Frederica Bimmel.

7. Approach to the Abyss

Part 1: The Evidence

Starling goes to Belvedere, Ohio to visit Fredrica Bimmel’s house. When she arrives, her father says that she went on a bus to Chicago to see about a job when she left the house. Starling goes up the staircase and enters Fredrica’s room. In her room, Starling finds a small box with polaroid pictures of Fredrica herself. Starling goes to the room across the hall, then finds mannequins and garments. She opens the closet, and notices a cloth cut-outs that have identical shapes as the skinned area of Frederica’s body. Starling immediately calls Crawford and reports the evidence and suggests that Buffalo Bill can be a skilled tailor or a dressmaker. She figures out that the reason he kidnaps big women is to get as much skin as possible, and to easily skin them by loosening their skin by starving them. Crawford calms Starling down, and says that the FBI have identified Buffalo Bill and his location, Calumet City, Illinois, and that they are on their way to capture him. His name is Jamie Gumm, known as John Grant. Crawford tells Starling that two years ago in LAX, he had records for importing live caterpillars from Asia. Crawford orders Starling to link the suspect to Fredrica Bimmel before his indictment. Starling follows his orders.

Part 2: Talk with the friend

Starling visits one of Fredrica’s friends to ask if they have acquaintance with a man named Jamie Gumm, or John Grant. The friend says that she never heard of the name, and that she didn’t have any guys around her because sewing was her life. She also says that Fredrica and her worked together to do alterations for Mrs. Lipman. Starling gets Mrs. Lipman’s address from the friend, and heads to her house located at Calumet City, Illinois. Starling gets prepared for the major challenge.

Part 3

Meanwhile, the FBI crash into Jamie Gumm’s house, but realizes that it’s an empty house.

8. The Ordeal/ Crisis/ Abyss

Starling arrives at Mrs. Lipman’s house, and rings the doorbell. However, instead of Mrs. Lipman, Buffalo Bill opens the door and says that the Lipmans don’t live at the house anymore. Starling stops Buffalo Bill from trying to close the door, and asks if he has any contact information of Mrs. Lipman. She also asks Buffalo Bill if he knows anything about Fredrica Bimmel. He replies that he saw her name in the newspaper. He then offers Starling to come into the house while he finds the card of Mrs. Lipman’s son. While Buffalo Bill searches for the card, Starling scans the house and notices several suspicious items such as a roll of large plastic bags, numerous rolls of thread and most importantly, the moth. Instantly, Starling points her gun at Buffalo Bill and yells him to freeze and put his hand over his head.

However, he manages to escape from Starling and runs down to his basement. Starling immediately follows him downstairs with her gun. She opens doors to several rooms and finds garments made out of human skin, and rotting corpses in bathtubs. Starling goes further into the basement, and finds the well where Catherine Martin is trapped. Starling, nervous but calmly tells Catherine that she has to leave the room to find and arrest Buffalo Bill.

Starling enters more rooms to locate the murderer, but the lights fade out. Starling confronts one of her greatest fears. She desperately sticks to the wall and searches the rooms. Meanwhile, Buffalo Bill stands very close to Starling with his night-vision goggles and silently watches Starling, who is breathing heavily in terror. Buffalo Bill follows behind Starling as she carries on her search. The moment Buffalo Bill loads his gun to shoot Starling at her back, she immediately turns around and shoots multiple rounds of bullets at Buffalo Bill. She was able to find him in the dark because of the sound of him loading his gun. Buffalo Bill dies immediately.

9. The Reward

Soon, the FBI arrives. Catherine Martin is rescued and Starling, still in fear, is joined by Jack Crawford. Their safety is now secured. They return back to Virginia.

10. The Road Back

Starling graduates from the FBI Academy and is now officially a special agent of the FBI. She is congratulated by closing the Buffalo Bill case by her friends and her boss, Jack Crawford.

11. The Resurrection

At the commencement ceremony, Starling gets a phone call from Hannibal Lecter.
“Starling.”
“Clarice. Have the lambs stopped screaming?”
“Dr. Lecter.”
“Don’t bother with the trace. I won’t be on long enough.”
“Where are you Dr. Lecter?”
“I have no plans of calling you, Clarice. The world’s more interesting with you in it. So you take care now to extend me the same courtesy.”
“You know I can’t make that promise.”
“I do wish we could chat longer but I am having an old friend for dinner. Bye.”
Hannibal gently warns Starling not to try to arrest him.

12. The Return with the Elixir

Starling knows that life won’t be the same after realizing Hannibal Lecter is roaming around.
